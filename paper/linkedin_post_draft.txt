LINKEDIN POST DRAFT v2 - no story arc, artifact-first
=====================================================

I machine-checked RSA, the encryption math behind most payments and
logins since the 70s - from absolute zero, in Lean 4.

196 lemmas across 12 tiers, starting at 0 + n = n and building through
Fermat's Little Theorem and the Chinese Remainder Theorem to the capstone:
a computer-verified proof that RSA decryption always recovers the original
message. The identity every RSA implementation on earth silently relies on.

No Mathlib. No external dependencies. Every brick in the tower is in the
repo and checked in a single build.

The image is the actual dependency graph - every dot a proven lemma, every
line one proof resting on another. The gold star is rsa_correctness.

You don't have to trust any of this, which is the point:

  git clone github.com/adventurelands/rsa-correctness-lean4
  lake build          -> the computer re-checks all 196 proofs
  grep -rn sorry      -> zero (Lean's "I gave up" keyword)

Scope, honestly: this proves textbook-RSA correctness, not implementation
security - padding, key generation, and side channels are a different war.
RSA has been formalized before in Coq/Isabelle/Lean+Mathlib; the point here
is zero dependencies, all the way down.

This is my first formal verification project that I'm putting in public -
feedback, edit suggestions, and harder questions are genuinely welcome,
especially from people who do this professionally.
